Understanding visa scams

Visa scams are deceptive schemes designed to exploit individuals unfamiliar with visa procedures.

Scammers often create fake websites that look like legitimate visa service providers, making distinguishing between real and fraudulent platforms difficult.

'I was tempted by a website offering an 'express visa' for just $50—way cheaper than the official fee. Everything looked legitimate until I noticed the website had no padlock in the address bar and the contact email was a generic Gmail account. That’s when I realized it was a scam and backed out just in time.' — James, USA.

Here’s how to recognize potential scams and avoid them:

Red flag What to look out for
Unrealistic promises and guarantees Watch out for sites guaranteeing approval or offering seemingly impossibly fast processing times
Unsafe payment or information requests Be wary of platforms requesting sensitive details via email
Lack of contact information Fake websites often have no contact numbers, customer service details, or support emails listed.
Unprofessional website design Poorly designed websites with errors or outdated layouts are usually untrustworthy.
No legitimate credentials or reviews Always check for Trustpilot ratings or verified customer reviews.

Protect yourself by ensuring the website is secure (look for 'https://' and a padlock icon) and comparing pricing with trusted sources like us.
